Heart Disease

A Preventable Illness

By Kevin Lin and Terrence Nguyen and Isabelle Woodruff-Madeira

In the US alone, it is estimated that half of adults currently live with hypertension, or better known as high blood pressure. Untreated hypertension can eventually lead to heart failure, which is responsible for about 379,800 deaths in 2018.

Along with hypervention, those with diabetes, hyperlipidaemia, and other cardiovascular diseases also have a high risk of heart failure. Preventive screenings and early detection are one major way to reduce the chances of heart failure, especially to those already at risk.

What is Heart Failure?

Heart Failure is a lifelong condition where the heart cannot pump enough blood for the minimum need of the body. Our bodies have different methods of handling this issue, from the heart enlarging or pumping faster to meet demand. These are all temporary solutions, and if left untreated will lead to death.

Currently, there are about 6.7 million Americans over the age of 20 that have heart failure.

Prediction Methods

The most straight forward way to screen for heart failure is with a physical examination. But if we were to prevent heart failure instead, it would be more complicated. The best method for screening the risks would be assessing the ejection fraction (EF) of one's heart. Ejection fraction is a measurement of how much blood the heart pumps within its left vesticle with each contraction. For scale, a normal ejection fraction would be between 50-70 percent of the total blood contained in the left vesticle is pumped out with each contraction.

An unhealthy EF would be less than 49 percent.

There are a few methods of treating heart failure, such as surgery or medication that directly treats the cause. But living a healthy lifestyle, avoiding tobacco and maintaining a healthy diet are all ways to prevent heart failure from occuring.

Research and the Future

Currently there are many exciting ways scientists and doctors are treating heart failure. Heart pumps, new genes and proteins, and man-made cells are all ways they are finding solutions. But the best solution you can have is preventing it from happening in the first place.

It is said that those with hypertension may need to increase their medication dosage. Relying on medication is not ideal and can get very expensive compared to maintaining a healthy lifestyle.

Good habits will lead to good results.

The more you know about your health and body, the better you can adjust to maintain it in a healthy way.
